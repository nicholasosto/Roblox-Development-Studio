# Veiled Administration

## Boundary / State / Relationship / Transition / Order

- **Boundary:** ceilings lower, windows narrow, and gold-coded inventory thresholds replace the upper Sanctum's expansive transparency.
- **State:** rooms can shift between benign administrative presentation and active field-control work through shutters, consoles, and access state.
- **Relationship:** the district occupies the lower mirror of upper healing and governance spaces, exposing the operational cost beneath them.
- **Transition:** public architectural rhythm persists long enough to feel familiar, then becomes compressed and procedural.
- **Order:** staff circulation, tuning rooms, logistics cells, record control, restricted transfer vestibule.

## Player path and sightlines

The route should feel watched without relying on obvious guards. Doors ahead remain visible but progressively stop clarifying; staff shortcuts create glimpses of processes the main path cannot reach.

## Roblox translation

Reuse strand modules with denser room inserts, opaque membranes, practical service lighting, and functional gold trim. Keep doors and access states explicit; no new stealth or surveillance mechanic is implied by this architectural record.

## Acceptance checks

- [ ] The district fits within the same strand envelope as its upper mirror.
- [ ] Primary route remains readable under darker lighting.
- [ ] At least two staff-only connections bypass the public-facing sequence.
- [ ] Gold trim consistently marks inventory/access functions.
- [ ] The space feels administrative, not a conventional dungeon.
